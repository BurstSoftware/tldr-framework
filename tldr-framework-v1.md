# TLDR Framework

A practical product framework for building **competitive daily/weekly summary & discovery tools** that help people explore what they care about online — without the noise.

**Goal**: Create a product people open every morning that feels like "the internet, but actually useful."

## 1. Core Philosophy (The TL;DR Spirit)

- **Too Long; Didn't Explore** → Deliver signal, kill noise.
- Respect time: Core experience ≤ 5–7 minutes.
- One clear value: "Here's what matters today in the topics you actually care about."

## 2. Target Use Cases

- Busy developers / tech professionals who want to stay current.
- Curious explorers (AI, science, business, culture, niche hobbies).
- Teams that want shared knowledge without meetings.
- Power users tired of algorithm-driven feeds (Twitter/X, Reddit, newsletters).

## 3. Key Product Pillars

### A. Personalization & Topics
- User selects **interest clusters** (not just broad categories):
  - Tech (AI, Web Dev, DevOps, Infra, Security…)
  - Science & Research
  - Business & Startups
  - Design / Product / Marketing
  - Culture, Open Source, Indie Hacking, etc.
- Allow custom topics + RSS/keyword following.
- Smart defaults + progressive onboarding.

### B. Content Curation Model
1. **Sources**: High-quality + long-tail (blogs, papers, GitHub, forums, indie sites, podcasts, YouTube).
2. **Signals**: Virality, recency, depth, authority, discussion quality.
3. **Human + AI Hybrid**:
   - AI for first-pass summarization & clustering.
   - Human editors / community moderators for taste & accuracy.
4. **Diversity**: Mix of mainstream + hidden-gem content.
